1 Abstract
Concept Component Analysis (ConCA) was originally introduced to study whether
hidden representations learned by large models admit a concept-oriented de-
composition under a probabilistic interpretation. In the original Sparse ConCA
formulation, each latent component is interpreted as an approximation to a
latent log-posterior quantity conditioned on a single representation. However,
many semantic phenomena in modern foundation models are inherently dis-
tributional rather than pointwise: concepts often emerge across paraphrases,
reasoning trajectories, or local families of hidden states. This motivates extend-
ing ConCA from single representations to representation sets. In this section,
we introduce Set-ConCA, a principled extension of Sparse ConCA that esti-
mates concept representations from local representation sets. The proposed
formulation preserves the probabilistic interpretation of ConCA while introduc-
ing permutation-invariant aggregation and subset consistency regularization,
enabling more stable concept learning under local representation distributions.

6 Why Set-ConCA Matters
Compared with Sparse ConCA, Set-ConCA extends concept learning from point
representations to local representation distributions.
This improves:
•concept stability,
•robustness to local sampling noise,
•invariance across paraphrases,
•suitability for reasoning trajectories.
7 Immediate Practical Implementation
A minimal practical implementation uses:
•linear encoder,
•mean pooling,
•shared + residual decoder,
•smooth probability surrogate,
•L1sparsity,
•subset consistency regularization.
No heavy generator is necessary in the first stage.
8 Future Extensions
Set-ConCA naturally supports future extensions:
•slot-based concept decomposition,
•hierarchical local-global concept learning,
•trajectory-level concept dynamics,
•posterior drift analysis for hallucination and safety.
This makes Set-ConCA a natural next step toward concept-level world mod-
eling in large language models.
